Annual Population Survey
Applies to people aged 16 to 64 in employment.
The skill level groups are created by grouping jobs together based on their occupation according to the Standard Occupation Classification (SOC) 2010 lower level groups.
Occupations were grouped by the skill level required according to the following guidelines:
High – This skill level is normally acquired through a degree or an equivalent period of work experience. Occupations at this level are generally termed ‘professional’
or managerial positions, and are found in corporate enterprises or governments. Examples include senior government officials, financial managers,
scientists, engineers, medical doctors, teachers and accountants.
High skill level data can be found in table 6, low skill level data can be found in table 7, upper-middle skill level data can be found in table 8 and lower-middle skill level data can be found in table 9.
Note that the total percentages of high, low, upper-middle and lower-middle skill levels may not sum to 100 as missing values are included in totals.
