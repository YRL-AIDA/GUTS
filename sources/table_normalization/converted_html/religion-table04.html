The skill level groups are created by grouping jobs together based on their occupation according to the Standard Occupation Classification (SOC) 2010 lower level groups.
Occupations were grouped by the skill level required according to the following guidelines:
Upper-middle – This skill level equates to competence acquired through post-compulsory education but not to degree level. Occupations found at this level include
a variety of technical and trades occupations, and proprietors of small business. For the latter, significant work experience may be typical.
Examples of occupations at this level include catering managers, building inspectors, nurses, police officers (sergeant and below), electricians and plumbers.

Note that the total percentages of high, low, upper-middle and lower-middle skill levels may not sum to 100 as missing values are included in totals.
